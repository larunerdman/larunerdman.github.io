Research Areas

Modes of Gene Expression Regulation

Gene expression is regulated by a compendium of direct and indirect effects from multiple different regulatory bodies in the cell. My work in this area has focused on isolating shared and independent effects of different regulators for the purpose of experimental hypothesis generation, clustering of genes by regulatory signatures, and comparison of regulation across tissues and disease.

Psychiatric Genetics

Our understanding of the genetics and pathophysiology of many psychiatric disorders is still in its nascency. This is primarily due to (1) the polygenic nature of most psychiatric disorders, (2) difficulty studying the brain in a controlled manner, and (3) heterogeneity of the symptomatology and deficits that individuals with psychiatric disorders face. My work in this area targets all three of these areas by studying the group effects of genetic variants annotated to putative causal pathways and incorporating deep phenotyping into analyses of neuroimaging and genetics data. The disorders I primarily focus on are obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD) and attention-deficit hyperactive disorder (ADHD).

Disentangling Complex Disease

The diagnostic criteria for many disorders groups a heterogenous set of patients under a singular label. This makes studying the genetics, pathophysiology, and treatment response of those patients challenging. My work in this area focuses on integrating data from a multitude of different data types relevant to the disease under investigation in order to identify more homogenous subtypes of individuals within the same or a similar disease group.
